About LeBron

From Akron to All-Time

Early Life

LeBron Raymone James was born on December 30, 1984, in Akron, Ohio. Raised by his mother Gloria James, LeBron grew up in challenging circumstances but found his calling on the basketball court. By the time he reached St. Vincent–St. Mary High School, he was already a national phenomenon — his games were broadcast on ESPN before he ever played a single NBA minute. Sports Illustrated put him on the cover as a 17-year-old with the headline "The Chosen One."

The NBA Career

Selected first overall by the Cleveland Cavaliers in the 2003 NBA Draft, LeBron wasted no time proving he was worth the hype. Over 23 seasons, he has played for four franchises and won a championship with three of them — the Miami Heat (2012, 2013), the Cleveland Cavaliers (2016), and the Los Angeles Lakers (2020). In February 2023, he became the NBA's all-time leading scorer, surpassing Kareem Abdul-Jabbar's record of 38,387 points. He has since surpassed 40,000 career points, becoming the first player in NBA history to reach that milestone.

The Defining Moment

No single play defines LeBron's legacy more than his chase-down block of Andre Iguodala in the final minutes of Game 7 of the 2016 NBA Finals. With Cleveland trailing the Golden State Warriors 3–1 in the series, LeBron delivered one of the most improbable championship comebacks in sports history, fulfilling his promise to bring a title to Northeast Ohio.

Beyond Basketball

LeBron James is more than an athlete. Through his LeBron James Family Foundation, he opened the I PROMISE School in Akron in 2018, providing at-risk children with education, meals, and family support services. In 2024, he made history again when he and his son Bronny became the first father-son duo to play together in an NBA game, suiting up alongside each other for the Los Angeles Lakers.
